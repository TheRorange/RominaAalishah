🧠 Machine Reasoning  ·  ⚡ Edge Computing  ·  👁 Computer Vision

I study the gap between looking intelligent and being intelligent. At Johns Hopkins, my work sits at the intersection of machine reasoning, computer vision, and model compression, investigating how neural networks learn, represent, and transfer knowledge, and what is preserved or lost when large models are made small. I'm drawn to problems where the engineering question and the scientific question turn out to be the same question.
